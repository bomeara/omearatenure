Lampyr biodiversity app

Lampyr is a web app that presents information on the biodiversity all around us. The framework was made by Brian O'Meara, an assistant professor in the department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville in order to better teach an intro biodiversity class, Bio130. An important feature of the app is that students, many of them freshmen, will contribute information about individual species as mini-essays over the semester. This will serve the same purpose as traditional assignments that teach students how to do research, how to cite, etc. while at the same time making sure their work can benefit others.

Details

This app is actively in development, with coding only starting on Dec. 20, 2011, so it lacks all planned features. It uses a simple MySQL database and PHP back end and jQuery Mobile front end. Source code is available here and we're quite open to having other developers (contact bomeara@utk.edu). A hope is that in the future other instructors may adopt this app and have their classes improve it, too. Data has come from Genbank, LifeMapper, GBIF (only those records that are permitted to be downloaded and rehosted), and the All Taxa Biodiversity Inventory, which is working to inventory all species in Great Smoky Mountains National Park. Code from Rod Page's iSpecies has been modified for Wikipedia parsing. The name "Lampyr" comes from Lampyridae, the clade of beetles better known as "fireflies." Great Smoky Mountains National Park is famous for its remarkable synchronized fireflies. The icon image comes from a photo by Terry Priest, released under a Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike license.
